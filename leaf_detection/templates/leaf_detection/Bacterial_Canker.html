BACTERIAL CANKER

Preventions

  • Use healthy seedlings/grafting material from sources free from the disease

  • Avoid transport of infected material to areas free from the disease

  • Prune and burn infected plant parts and old mango fruits and fruit stalk after harvest

  • Prune trees regularly to improve sunlight penetration and air movement by removing internal part of canopy and disease branches

  • Pruning equipment should surface sterilized with 5% bleach or sodium hypochlorite as pruners move from one trees to another

  • Avoid all cultivation activities when the trees are wet as the disease spreads more quickly when conditions are wet

Monitoring

  • Monitor weekly for symptoms of the disease
  • On leaves the infection starts as small brown oily spots that turn necrotic, black, angular raised spots sometimes surrounded by a yellow halo
  • Branches, petioles and perduncles show cracks, raised spots and cankers. A sticky exudate (gum) exudes from the cankers mainly in the rainy season
  • On fruits small black raised spots form often teardrop shaped. These spots enlarge, crack and sticky gum ooze from the cracks
  • Act when the first symptoms are seen or copper fungicides can be used as a preventative measure
  • Lesions of black spot are more angular and raised than anthracnose but very similar

Direct Control

  • Prune and burn all infected branches to reduce the spread of the disease
  • Destroy infected fruit both on the tree and ground regulary to avoid spread
  • Remove seriously disease trees, when cankers are present on the main trunk
